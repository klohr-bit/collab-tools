Six minutes. Four purposes. One foundation.

A great warm-up does four things at once. Most warm-ups do one — usually the physical part — and call it a day. Here's what you just walked through:

Physical
Each minute is harder than the last. Cognitive load climbs alongside the physical load.
Technical
Every minute trains a real skill — dead first touch, deceleration, scanning, passing on the move.
Social / Verbal
The 2v1 makes communication an explicit, named skill — with the same feedback questions every time.
Mental
Two balls, constant decisions, head on a swivel. Nobody zones out.

If this is the only thing you take from the Collab and try with your own group — try this. Run it once a week, ask the same two questions every time, and watch what happens to your athletes' communication over a month.

Why this lesson? Most coaches read about a warm-up and try it once. The ones that stick are the ones you understand well enough to defend — to athletes, to parents, to yourself on a tired Tuesday. This is the warm-up, with the why baked into every minute.
