The key takeaway
Players who use W.H.A.T. do not wait to be told what is happening. They observe during play, process between breaks, and communicate what they see to their teammates and coach. That is what Hockey IQ looks like from the inside. The more you practice reading the game this way, the faster it becomes automatic.
Take this to practice
At your next scrimmage, pick one thing to observe every time the opponent has the ball. Just one — their first pass direction, where their forwards position, how their backs outlet. Run your own W.H.A.T. at the quarter break and share what you noticed with a teammate. Start small. Build the habit.
