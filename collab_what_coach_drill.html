The key takeaway
W.H.A.T. is not a post-game tool. It is a between-quarters tool. The goal is to move through all four steps efficiently enough to make a clear decision before the next period starts. Observe during the game. Review and brainstorm at the break. Decide and communicate before play resumes. Train it at practice so it becomes automatic in games.
Take this to practice
Run a scrimmage and pause it at the end of each period. Give your players 60 seconds to run through W.H.A.T. as a team before play resumes. Make it a habit at practice and it will show up in games without prompting.
