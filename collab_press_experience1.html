What Is a Press — and What Is It Trying to Do?
Before you can read a press, you need to understand its job.
The core concept
Every press your team runs — and every press you face — is built around two ideas. Lock and Key. Understanding these two things makes every other concept click into place.
🔒
Lock
The team defense. The system that shuts down space and stops the opponent from reaching the goal. A lock is secure when every player knows their role and executes it.
🔑
Key
The individual and team tools used to unlock the opponent's press. Keys find the space, create the angle, and move the ball through a press that is trying to stop you.
Why this matters for you as a player
When your team is defending, you are executing the Lock. When your team has the ball, you are looking for the Key. Every decision you make on the field fits into one of these two categories. The better you understand both, the faster your decisions become.
How the press uses the court
A press is not random. It is a deliberate system that uses the court's boundaries as additional defenders. The boards, the baseline, and the circle all become part of the Lock.

The player's job
When you have the ball, your job is to find the Key space — the open areas the press is not protecting — and move the ball there before the Lock closes it. When you do not have the ball, your job is to move into positions that force the press to make decisions.
Two types of press
Every press your team will face falls into one of two categories — or a hybrid of both. Knowing which one you are facing changes what Keys you look for.
Zone press — defenders cover areas, not players
Zone press — what to look for
Each defender owns an area, not a person. The Key is to move the ball faster than the zones can shift. Look for the gaps between zones — the seams. Quick ball movement and shape changes create overloads that zones cannot cover.
Man to Man press — each defender follows a specific player
Man to Man press — what to look for
Each defender is assigned to a specific player on your team. The Key is to create space through movement. When you move, your defender has to follow — that creates opportunities for teammates. Leading passes into space, midfield movement, and overloads all unlock M2M.
